# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Pokemon card collectors and small resellers who track their collection's value and actively buy/sell for profit. They open the app to check prices, review portfolio performance, log trades, and find upcoming card shows. The line between "collector" and "flipper" is blurry — the same user switches modes depending on the day.

## Product Purpose

PokePrice is a desktop-native portfolio tracker and price intelligence tool for Pokemon card enthusiasts. It lets users track graded and raw card values, watch price movements over time, analyze trades, and stay on top of local card shows — all in one place. Success looks like a user who can open the app, get a clear read on their portfolio's current value, and make a confident buy/sell decision in under two minutes.

## Brand Personality

Premium, bold, collector-proud. The card hobby is serious and valuable; the software should reflect that. Think Robinhood/Coinbase energy — financial confidence, clean data presentation, charts as a first-class citizen — applied to a hobby people genuinely love. The app should feel like it was built by someone who cares about their collection, not a generic fintech template.

## Anti-references

- **Cheap fan sites**: pixel fonts, card clipart, busy patterned backgrounds, the hobbyist-blog aesthetic. PokePrice is a serious tool.
- **Generic SaaS dashboards**: cream/sand backgrounds, teal or purple accents, identical icon-plus-text card grids repeated endlessly. Indistinguishable from any other B2B tool.
- **Childlike or overly playful**: the Pokemon brand is colorful; this app should feel grown-up and confident, not like a fan app.

## Design Principles

1. **Data earns its place.** Every number, chart, and label should help the user make a decision. No decorative metrics.
2. **Confidence, not clutter.** The dark theme and amber accent are intentional signals of focus. Don't add color for its own sake.
3. **Collector-grade polish.** The people using this care deeply about condition, grading, and value. The UI should match that level of care — precise, well-spaced, never rough.
4. **Speed over ceremony.** The primary workflow is check price → decide. Minimize friction between opening the app and getting an answer.
5. **One source of truth.** The portfolio view is the anchor. Everything else (watchlist, trade analyzer, card shows) supports it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No specific WCAG target. Prioritize excellent usability for sighted, mouse-driven users. Maintain readable contrast on the dark theme — the deep navy background makes low-contrast text a real risk.
